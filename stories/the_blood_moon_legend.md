# The Blood Moon Legend

The Blood Moon is a rare event in the world above, but beneath the dungeon it is a constant promise. The dark forces that birthed the dungeon were born under a red sky. The moon is said to have bled into the earth and dyed the first coins with its color. Even when the moon is bright above, the dungeon remembers that blood. The walls below still glow faintly as if they have been lit by that same red light. The creatures that roam the halls are more restless in that glow. The legend says the dungeon itself is a wounded thing, and the Blood Moon is the scab that keeps it closed. It is a promise and a threat together.

Legend says that when the moon turns red, the dungeon’s gold glows brighter and the monsters move faster. The shadows lengthen, and the whispers of forgotten debts become louder. The air grows thick with the scent of iron and damp earth. Adventurers say the light of their lanterns looks different under the Blood Moon. Their footsteps echo farther. The red glow seems to find every coin and every secret. Monsters that normally sleep will awake and follow the sound of their own hearts. The Blood Moon does not just affect the dungeon; it changes those who walk within it.

Some adventurers believe the Blood Moon can be used to bargain with the dungeon. They leave offerings of gold and memories, hoping to earn a safe passage through the deepest corridors. The offerings are not always coins; sometimes they are a story, a regret, or a promise. The dungeon accepts some offerings and rejects others. When it accepts, the Blood Moon light seems to become a guide rather than a threat. Those who are wise offer only what they can afford to lose. Those who are greedy are swallowed by the hunger. The bargain is ancient and it is exacting. It does not forgive lies.

Others think the Blood Moon is a warning: take no more than you need, or the dungeon will take back far more than coins. The red light is said to reveal the true price of every treasure. If you move forward with greed in your eyes, the halls close around you. If you move forward with respect, the shadows part and the path becomes clearer. Many adventurers have survived the Blood Moon only to lose their friends on the next floor. The warning is not only for what you take, but for what you leave behind. The dungeon remembers debts, both paid and unpaid. The Blood Moon is the ledger that refuses to be balanced.

Only a few stories are told about those who understood the Blood Moon. They speak of a ritual performed in a hall lit entirely in red. They speak of a stone altar that soothed the hunger with a single honest confession. They speak of a coin offered without expectation. Some say the dungeon itself can be calmed for a moment if you accept the moon’s gift without demanding more. Others say the Blood Moon will always be a test that never ends. The legend is a reminder that the deepest power in the dungeon is not the gold, but the choice that comes with it. The moon is a promise of danger and the possibility of mercy.
