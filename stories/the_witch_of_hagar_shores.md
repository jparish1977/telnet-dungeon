# The Witch of Hagar Shores

Hagar Shores was once a quiet fishing village on a rocky coast. The villagers spoke quietly of a woman who lived in the salt marshes and traded spells for memories. Her hut was built from driftwood and seaweed, and lanterns swung from the crooked rafters. Children were warned not to cross the tidal flats after sunset. The witch was said to know the name of every fish, bird, and storm cloud. She gathered tears from the ocean and wove them into charms. People came to her with broken hearts as often as with broken nets. She listened in a voice that sounded like waves on stone.

When the dungeon rose, the witch vanished into the mist. Some say she became the guardian of the first floor, bending the sea mist into illusions and luring foolish travelers into traps. Others claim she simply moved deeper, following the path of the curse. The trolls and sirens that now haunt that floor still carry her touch. She is credited with the strange protection runes scratched into the walls near the coastal descent. Those runes sometimes glow when a traveler makes a kind choice. The witch’s bargains are never easy, but they are always honest. She does not lie about the price.

The witch's offerings are strange: a potion that restores life at a terrible cost, a charm that lets you hear monsters through walls, and a warning carved in old driftwood. Her potions are brewed with salt, bone, and a single memory plucked from the drinker. The charm she offers whispers where danger lies, but it also recalls every fearful moment you have ever felt. The warning carved in driftwood reads like a prayer and a threat at the same time. It says that the sea remembers the debt owed by those who take from it. The witch does not value gold; she values the stories people carry. Every bargain is a thread in the tapestry of the shore. The witch makes no bargains lightly, and the cost is always a story worth keeping.

Adventurers who survive her trials return with stories of a pale figure watching from the surf, her staff shaped like a twisted fishbone. She is not simply a monster; she remembers what the world was like before the darkness. She speaks of the old village and the first time the land shook. She asks those who have been below the dungeon to tell her what they saw. Some say the witch will grant safe passage back to the shore if you answer honestly. Others say she will steal your best memory in exchange for a favor. The witch is both guardian and gatekeeper. Her presence reminds travelers that not all power in the dungeon is born of greed.

Some adventurers say that if you stand on the shore at dawn, you can hear the witch speaking to the tides themselves. She seems to know when the dungeon shifts and when the sea will reclaim stones. Her warnings are often the only thing that keeps sailors from wandering into the cursed surf. She does not ask for tribute from everyone; she asks for a promise that is kept. That promise is the one thing the shore has left aside from salt and memory. Those who break it find the waves turn cold and the fog close in. Those who honor it may find a hidden path back to safety. The witch’s voice is the last blessing for those who survive the shore and the first curse for those who do not.
