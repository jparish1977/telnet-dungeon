# Niles: The Frontier Town

Niles sits on the contested border between settled territory and wild lands. It's a town of merchants, prospectors, and adventure-seekers who are too wild for Buchanan's order or too cautious to venture into the farthest reaches alone.

## Geography

Built at the edge of a massive cliff face, Niles overlooks a vast chasm. The **Niles Chasm Dungeon** entrance is unusual: it requires rappelling or falling to reach (there are platforms with safety nets). The view from Niles is spectacular and unsettling — the chasm stretches for miles in both directions, and nothing lives in its depths.

**Key Locations:**
- **The Chasm Edge** — The precipice where the dungeon entrance can be seen
- **The Rope House** — Where climbers and rappelers gear up before descents
- **The Prospector's Exchange** — A rough trading post where adventurers barter rare finds
- **The Deep Watch** — A watchtower where... watchers observe the chasm. No one knows who they are or who they report to.
- **The Codex Second Archive** — A branch library where Codex Society scholars study chasm-related phenomena

## History & Culture

### Built on Mystery

Niles was originally founded by miners seeking minerals. They found rich seams but also found something else: ancient carvings on the chasm walls suggesting the chasm was *carved deliberately*. By whom? Why?

This discovery made Niles a destination for explorers and scholars as much as miners. The town is a nexus of conflicting interests: miners want profit, scholars want knowledge, adventurers want glory, and someone in The Deep Watch wants secrets kept.

### The Watchers

No one admits to working for The Deep Watch or knowing its purpose. But people occasionally disappear, and their items show up in mysterious auctions. NPCs who ask too many questions about the Watch get nervous and change the subject.

## Present Day

Niles is more dangerous than other towns, but also more exciting. The atmosphere is one of frontier freedom mixed with underlying tension. Everyone has secrets. Everyone is looking for something.

## Rumors & Hooks

- Miners have hit something massive and metallic deep below the chasm. It doesn't match any known Elder architecture. The minerals around it glow faintly.
- A scholar claimed to discover that the chasm is aligned astrologically with a pattern seen in Elder star charts. She disappeared after publishing her findings.
- The Rope House owner has been buying old climbing equipment - far more than needed. For what expedition?
- Someone's been poisoning the well water in the poor district. Victims report strange dreams and visions of vast underground chambers.
- The Codex Second Archive director meets regularly with someone from The Deep Watch. They argue in hushed tones.

## Unique Mechanics

**Gravity Wells** — The Niles Chasm Dungeon has sections where gravity is reversed or weakened. Adventurers can "fall upward" or hover. This creates unique navigation puzzles.

**Chasm Echoes** — Every sound echoes through the chasm, distorting over distance and time. This is used for communication and navigation but also creates disorienting effects for unprepared adventurers.

**The Glow** — Certain depths in the chasm are luminescent. This natural light makes navigation easier, but certain creatures are attracted to it. Glowing attracts predators.

## Hooks for Adventurers

- Someone is paying good coin for expeditions into *specific* parts of the chasm - those with the metallic object
- The scholar who disappeared left behind a journal with half-finished theories
- The Deep Watch is actively recruiting, though they don't advertise
- Miners have hit something that seems to have *woken up* - unusual creatures are emerging from the depths
