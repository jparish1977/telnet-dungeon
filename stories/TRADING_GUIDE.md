# The Trading System: Sharing Wealth

## Overview

The Dungeon of Doom supports a player-driven economy where adventurers can transfer wealth, share resources, and coordinate efforts. This system encourages cooperation and provides flexibility for players with different playstyles.

## How to Trade

### Accessing the Trading Hub

**Command:** `E` (Exchange)

At any time while in the game world, press `E` to open the **Trading Hub**. This interface lets you:

- Drop gold on the ground for other players  
- Pick up gold that others have dropped
- Trade directly with players on the same tile
- View gold piles at your location

### Dropping Gold

When you open the Trading Hub, you can select `[D]rop gold`. You'll be prompted to enter an amount. The gold leaves your inventory immediately and is visible to other players on your tile.

**Uses:**
- Helping new players get started
- Leaving wealth for a friend while they're in the dungeon
- Splitting dungeon loot with allies
- Paying premiums for rare items or services

### Picking Up Gold

If there's gold on the ground at your location, select `[P]ick up gold` and enter an amount. You'll acquire that much gold (up to what's available).

**Mechanics:**
- Multiple gold piles can exist at one location (from different players)
- When you pick up, the system draws from oldest piles first
- There's no weight limit on gold

### Direct Player Trading

If another player is on the same tile as you, you can `[T]rade with` them. This opens a 1-on-1 exchange interface where:

1. You make an offer of gold
2. They make an offer of gold
3. When both are ready, confirm the trade
4. Gold transfers instantly
5. Both players are notified

**Mechanics:**
- Direct trades are **final** — once accepted, the exchange happens
- You can see the other player's name and level before deciding
- Messages appear to both players when a trade completes
- A broadcast message is sent to the floor: "X and Y completed a trade!"

## Strategic Uses

### Gold Pooling for Guild Wars

Groups of players can pool resources before a major dungeon expedition. One player might have the equipment, another provides the potion funding, a third handles healing supplies.

### Charity & Mentorship

Experienced players can drop gear rewards or starter gold to help new adventurers get on their feet. This builds community goodwill and encourages players to help each other progress.

### Black Market Operations

Discretion optional — players can trade in "hot" items or services. The system doesn't prevent this, but it does create a visible global record ("X and Y completed a trade").

### Inter-Faction Support

Different quests and factions might offer rewards. Adventurers can consolidate resources across characters or align with specific factions' quest goals by pooling purchases.

### Shared Expeditions

Before entering a challenging dungeon floor, allies coordinate funding for healing potions, equipment upgrades, or quest-specific items. The expedition succeeds or fails as a team, and resources are pooled accordingly.

## World Economy Notes

### Gold is Persistent

Dropped gold remains on the ground indefinitely. There's no despawn timer. This means:

- Wealthy players can leave "treasure caches" in specific locations
- Markets can form at crossroads where players congregate
- Gold left on a town floor acts as a secure storage location (relatively)

### Trading Creates Lore

Every trade is a social event. Broadcasts are seen by the whole floor, creating informal gossip and reputation:

- "Saw Thorgrim and Silvia trading a ton of gold — must be running something big"
- "Guthren keeps dropping gold in the chasm — he's either rich or desperate"
- "The Goblins' Bane party always trades together on their returns"

### Markets Will Emerge

While the core shop (weapons, armor, potions) is fixed, the *player market* for specific items, services, or group participation will form naturally:

- Adventurers might hang out in towns and offer guide services ("I'll lead you to X for 500g")
- Specialized players might "trade" information ("I have mapping data for sale")
- Rare dungeon items might be auctioned or traded between players

## Technical Rules

### Gold Caps

- Maximum gold per character: **9,999,999 gold**
- You cannot trade more gold than you have
- Gold is capped on pickup (you can't exceed 9,999,999)

### Trading Locations

- You can trade anywhere in the game world (overworld, dungeon floors, quest dungeons)
- Both players must be on the same location (tile) to trade directly
- Gold can be dropped anywhere

### Broadcast Visibility

- All trades generate visible broadcast messages
- Everyone on the floor sees "X and Y completed a trade!"
- Dropped gold notifications appear: "X dropped amount g at coordinates!"
- These create a transparent economy — reputation matters

## Best Practices

### For Groups

- Designate a "treasurer" to manage pooled resources
- Establish intra-group trade rules before expeditions
- Use the broadcast system to signal when resources are being offered

### For Merchants

- Establish a "shop" location where you consistently post prices
- Use dropped gold as a display of wealth and trustworthiness
- Build reputation over time through fair trades

### For New Players

- Don't be afraid to ask for help in chat
- Experienced players often drop starter gold for new adventurers
- Joining a group pools resources and knowledge

### For Everyone

- Gold is social and creates stories
- Your trades are public — act with awareness that others are watching
- The community's economy is what everyone makes it

## FAQ

**Can I store gold by dropping it in one place?**

Yes! Drop it on a floor you control or with a trusted friend guarding it. It won't despawn.

**What if someone steals my gold?**

Dropped gold is fair game. If you leave it unguarded, others can pick it up. Use your imagination to keep it safe (leave it in a secure location, trade with trusted allies, keep it in your inventory).

**Can I reverse a trade?**

No. Trades are final once both parties accept. Be sure before confirming.

**What's the economy floor?**

There isn't one. But naturally, farming dungeons and boss fights will generate the most gold. High-level players farming bosses will have more wealth than casual players.

**Should I join a guild?**

Guilds aren't formally in the game, but groups of players can organize informally using the chat and trading system. Many communities naturally form around shared goals or playstyles.
