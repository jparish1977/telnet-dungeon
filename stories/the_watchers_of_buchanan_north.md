# The Watchers of Buchanan North

The Watchers of Buchanan North are a group of stone sentinels that stand along the ridge above the lost kingdom. They were carved by a long-forgotten order of guardians who vowed to watch the horizon for signs of danger. Their eyes are inlaid with fragments of green glass that catch the moonlight. The Watchers were never meant to move, but they are said to turn their heads slightly when storm clouds gather. Farmers once left offerings at their feet to thank them for safe seasons. The Watchers listened to songs carried by the wind and held the memory of the land’s old borders. Their presence made the people of Buchanan feel safer, even on the darkest nights.

After the curse fell, the Watchers remained standing while the world below changed. Their stone faces grew moss and their glass eyes dimmed, but the air around them stayed charged with a strange energy. Travelers who pass beneath them sometimes swear they hear a low hum, like the sound of distant bells. The Watchers now guard not only the ridge, but also the border between the old world and the dungeon’s first floor. Those who approach with respect are offered a silent blessing in the form of clear weather or steady footing. Those who approach with hostility feel the ground shift under their feet. The ridge is a place where the stone remembers the old promises of protection. It also remembers how those promises were broken.

The Watchers are said to be connected to an underground chamber beneath their feet, where the wisdom of the old order is preserved. This chamber can be reached by finding the hidden path behind the easterly sentinel. The path is marked by carvings of feathers and vines that glow faintly at dusk. Inside the chamber are tablets carved with the names of those who once protected Buchanan. The tablets also contain instructions for healing the land if the right ritual is performed. The ritual calls for a gift of light, a prayer of peace, and a pledge to honor the Watchers’ vigil. Those who succeed in the ritual are said to feel the stone around them hum with pleasure.

The Watchers also bear a warning. They remember the day when the proudest family in Buchanan crossed the ridge to seek riches in the dungeon. The family never returned, and the Watchers’ faces seem to harden at the memory. The warning carved into the nearest sentinel reads: "Do not seek to take more than you have carried into the dark." This warning is not directed only at gold, but at pride, hatred, and despair. The Watchers have watched many foolish travelers and very few wise ones. They know that the greatest danger is not the dungeon itself, but the person who enters it without a way to come back changed for the better.

Those who visit the Watchers of Buchanan North often leave with a sense of solemn peace. They speak of a ridge where the air is clear and the stones seem to breathe. They tell of a hidden chamber below that holds the old order’s last instructions. The Watchers’ story is one of memory and promise, not merely of vigilance. It reminds adventurers that the dungeon is part of a world that also has guardians and old loyalties. The Watchers themselves are a story carved in stone, and they continue to keep watch over the land even now.
