# Advanced Lore: Factional Politics & Hidden Agendas

## The Factional Landscape

The dungeon towns are a nexus of conflicting interests. On the surface, the Adventurer's Guilds regulate exploration and shops provide gear. But beneath, powerful organizations maneuver for control, knowledge, and power.

## Factional Breakdown

### The Orthodox Codex Society

**Leadership:** High Master Aldrin (Buchanan), with councils in Coloma and Niles

**Philosophy:** 
- Knowledge belongs to the well-educated elite
- Dangerous truths should be carefully managed
- The Elder Civilization was great but flawed
- The Sundering was likely natural disaster misattributed to magic

**Public Face:**
- Maintains libraries and archives
- Funds scholarly expeditions
- Publishes research and maintains academic standards
- Seen as trustworthy and authoritative

**Secret Goals:**
- Control what information enters circulation
- Suppress theories that threaten their authority
- Acquire Elder artifacts before anyone else can
- Monitor adventurers for signs of power or knowledge they deem dangerous

**Operations:**
- Run the Scholar's Archives in towns
- Employ field researchers who blend in as adventurers
- Have informants in every major guild chapter
- Control the narrative through publications

### The Heretical Codex (The Underground)

**Leadership:** Archivist Tomas and an unnamed coalition

**Philosophy:**
- The public deserves the truth
- The Sundering was *orchestrated*, not natural
- The Elder Civilization left messages for descendants
- The current authorities are suppressing reality

**Public Face:**
- Non-existent; they're deliberately hidden
- Seen as conspiracy theorists by orthodox members
- Some sympathize but are afraid to openly align

**Secret Goals:**
- Publish the "true" history of The Sundering
- Locate evidence of deliberate sabotage in ancient times
- Build a coalition that can challenge the Orthodox authority
- Access the Forbidden Library and share its secrets

**Operations:**
- Recruit sympathetic scholars
- Operate safe houses for radical academics
- Publish anonymous papers that challenge orthodox findings
- Fund independent adventurer expeditions

### The Church of the Sundered Flame

**Leadership:** High Priestess Miriam (Buchanan chapter)

**Philosophy:**
- The Sundering was a divine test
- Dungeon delving is sacred work
- Courage and faith matter more than knowledge
- The divine has purposes mortals should trust

**Public Face:**
- Visible temples in major towns
- Bless adventurers before expeditions (morale boost)
- Assist the poor and injured
- Preach tolerance and faith

**Secret Goals:**
- Find religious artifacts in dungeon depths
- Prove the divine nature of The Sundering
- Convert people to their faith through adventure stories
- Use faith as a tool to ensure loyalty among believers

**Operations:**
- Temple networks across all towns
- Mission work among adventurers
- Fund faith-based expeditions
- Offer guidance and support to adventurers in exchange for tales of their expeditions

**Schism:**
- Some radical members believe The Sundering was *punishment* and are seeking ways to prevent "the next one"
- Others believe The Sundering is *foreordained* and continue to happen

### The Deepwarriors Guild

**Leadership:** Commander Kess (Niles chapter, strongest presence)

**Philosophy:**
- Strength and martial prowess are paramount
- Dungeon delving is conquest
- The weak serve the strong
- Power should be centralized for efficiency

**Public Face:**
- Legitimate mercenary outfit
- Offer bodyguard services to wealthy merchants
- Run training camps for warriors
- Appear as just another adventuring organization

**Secret Goals:**
- Build a military force to control dungeon entrance towns
- Monopolize access to the deepest, most powerful dungeons
- Find Elder weapons to maintain technological advantage
- Establish a martial oligarchy

**Operations:**
- Recruit top combatants with promises of glory and power
- Form elite squadrons
- Run private dungeons expeditions (no sharing loot)
- Intimidate or eliminate independent adventurers who interfere

**Tensions:**
- Actively hostile to The Underground (competing for power)
- In political struggle with Buchanan's Adventurer's Guild (which they see as weak)
- Secretly acquiring weapons-grade Elder artifacts

### The Merchant's Conspiracy (The Shadow Market)

**Leadership:** The Widow (true name unknown, headquarters unknown)

**Philosophy:**
- Money is power
- Information is commodity
- Control supply chains to control people
- Neutrality is profitable

**Public Face:**
- Individual merchants operating in various towns
- Black market dealers in "questionable" goods
- Information brokers
- Money lenders and bankers

**Secret Goals:**
- Create a parallel economy independent of official guild authorities
- Accrue wealth to eventually control power
- Trade in artifacts and secrets without regulation
- Build enough leverage to effectively run the towns from behind

**Operations:**
- Operate hidden markets in basement areas
- Fund adventurers doing "unofficial" work
- Broker information between factions (selling to highest bidder)
- Loan money at variable interest based on ability
- Handle black market goods (stolen items, forbidden artifacts, etc.)

**Secrets:**
- The Widow might be multiple people with the same codename
- They have connections to every faction
- They sell to all sides (no loyalty, only profit)
- Some whisper they have their own artifact hoard

### The Wardens of Equilibrium

**Leadership:** Unknown (seen only by faction members)

**Philosophy:**
- Some things must NOT be disturbed
- Balance must be maintained
- Certain knowledge is too dangerous
- Fate should not be tempered with

**Public Face:**
- Non-existent; unknown to most people
- Members appear as normal adventurers
- Occasionally intervene quietly in events

**Secret Goals:**
- Prevent catastrophic discoveries
- Neutralize threats to dimensional stability
- Monitor breach expansion
- Keep The Keeper and depths sealed/hidden

**Operations:**
- Members are scattered and operate independently
- Communicate through dead drops and codes
- Sometimes subtly sabotage expeditions heading into dangerous depths
- Have alliances with legitimate authorities (Church, Codex Society)

**Mystery:**
- No knows who leads them
- They might be an Elder AI construct still active
- Or descendants of the Elder Civilization
- Or something else entirely

## Factional Tensions

### The Core Conflict

**Orthodox Codex vs. The Underground:** A cold war over the truth about The Sundering. Escalating as evidence mounts.

**Deepwarriors vs. Everyone:** Growing militarism threatens other factions' interests. Could destabilize the region.

**Church vs. Orthodox Codex:** Different foundational beliefs. The Church sees divine will; Codex sees natural processes.

**Shadow Market vs. All Factions:** By refusing to take sides, The Widow profits from everyone but also makes enemies.

### Adventurer Implications

Depending on faction alignment:

- **Codex Member:** Access to libraries and research. Risk of being asked to suppress dangerous knowledge.
- **Church Believer:** Moral support and blessings. Risk of being sent on faith-based quests others might refuse.
- **Deepwarrior:** Combat training and warrior allies. Risk of forced participation in military objectives.
- **Shadow Market Client:** Access to forbidden goods and secret quests. Risk of legal trouble if caught.
- **Unaligned:** Freedom but no factional support. Other adventurers might not trust you.

## The Economy of Power

### Information Economy

- Adventurers can trade information they discover to competing factions
- The same discovery might be worth different amounts to different groups
- The Widow barters in information as much as gold

### The Quest Economy

- Different factions offer quests with different rewards and ethics
- A single location might support multiple incompatible quests
- Completing quests for one faction might lock out others

### The Artifact Economy

- Elder artifacts are the most valuable commodity
- Different factions seek different artifacts for different purposes
- Control of artifacts = control of dungeon exploration power

## Neutral Zones

**Overworld Towns:** Towns on the overworld are relatively neutral; factions maintain minimal presence.

**Guild Halls:** Official Guild chapters maintain strict neutrality rules within guild halls.

**Church Temples:** Churches explicitly neutral ground where all are welcome.

**The Taverns:** Places like Nyssa's bar in Buchanan are neutral social spaces where factions maintain minimal conflict.

## The Deeper Truth

There are hints that *all* factions are being played by something deeper:

- The Keeper might be aware of all factional movements
- Certain "accidents" seem too convenient to be natural
- Some adventurers report strange dreams with consistent themes across faction lines
- The appearance and strengthening of breach sites suggests active planning by *something*

Is there another power above all factions, orchestrating events? Or is it paranoia?

**The answer, adventurers will discover, depends on how deep they're willing to go.**
