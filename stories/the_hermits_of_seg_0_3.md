# The Hermits of Seg 0 3

The Hermits of Seg 0 3 are said to have lived in a ring of ancient pines on the northern edge of the overworld. They left their huts of woven branches and moss to wander the forest, collecting herbs and watching the stars. Their lives were quiet and simple, and they spoke rarely to traders. The hermits were known for making salves that could cure fever and for telling stories about the sky. Children were told to respect the border of their wood, because the hermits’ eyes were everywhere. The hermits believed that the forest itself was a living thing, and they treated every plant with reverence. They carried small lanterns that burned without smoke, and the light inside seemed to pulse like a heartbeat.

When the dungeon opened near Seg 0 3, the hermits did not flee. Instead, they built a hidden path into the twisting roots that connected the forest to the deeper floors. They placed charms on the trees and taught the forest how to whisper warnings to those who walked beneath it. The path is hard to find because the trees shift their branches when they sense someone with greed. The hermits’ lanterns still glow faintly among the roots, guiding travelers who remember their stories. The forest along the path is not hostile, but it is watchful. Its animals will follow you silently, and the wind will change direction if you take the wrong turn. Those who move through the path with respect say they feel the forest breathing around them.

The hermits of Seg 0 3 are also said to have hidden a library of dried herbs and handwritten journals in a hollow tree. The journals contain recipes for potions that can soothe pain and clear the mind. They also contain warnings about the dungeon’s more dangerous spirits. The hollow tree will open only for someone who offers a token of the forest: a leaf, a feather, or the memory of a moment of quiet kindness. The journals are not easy to read because the handwriting shifts with the light. Yet those who persevere can learn remedies that no ordinary shop carries. The hermits believed that healing was not just for the body, but also for the spirit.

The path from Seg 0 3 is guarded by a spirit called the Pine Watcher, a figure made of bark and mist. The Pine Watcher does not attack, but it tests travellers with questions about their journey. It asks whether they seek to learn, to heal, or to take. It can sense whether your heart is heavy with anger or light with curiosity. If you answer truthfully, it will point the way through the tangled roots. If you answer with malice, it will let the roots close behind you. The hermits taught the Forest of Seg 0 3 that the Watcher is not a gate to fear, but a guardian of balance.

Those who leave the path of the hermits often say that the forest remembers their names. They speak of lantern light that seemed to reach into their skin and of the quiet comfort of walking beneath old branches. The story of the Hermits of Seg 0 3 is a reminder that the dungeon is not only a place of stone and shadow, but also a world of living nature that can protect those who respect it. Travelers who remember the hermits’ teachings carry that respect forward into the deeper floors. They find that the forest they leave behind grows a little brighter because of it.
