# The Tower of the Whispering Winds

The Tower of the Whispering Winds once stood on a lonely ridge, miles from the nearest town. It was built to catch the wind and turn it into music that healed the weary. At its peak, the tower had a balcony that looked out over the sea and the far plains. Monks lived there, tending flutes and chimes that rang day and night. Travelers who passed by could hear the wind singing through the tower’s slats, and they felt calmed by its gentle melody. The tower was a place of meditation and study of the weather spirits. It was said that the winds themselves would bring news from distant places to those who listened.

When the curse reached the ridge, the tower did not fall. Instead, it became a place where wind and shadow met. The wind still blew, but it carried voices now. They whisper secrets and memories, sometimes offering warnings and sometimes teasing those who enter. The chimes at the top of the tower tinkle with a sound that can make your teeth hurt if you listen too long. The passages inside are narrow and spiraling, and the air is thick with dust and old incense. The monks who once lived there are gone, but their presence can still be felt in the corners. The tower seems to know when someone is afraid, and the wind blows harder in response.

The tower is protected by a spirit called the Windward Keeper, who appears as a figure made of ribbons and breeze. The Keeper does not speak in ordinary words; it communicates through the rhythm of the winds. It tests visitors by asking them to stand quietly and listen to the tower’s song. If you are patient and open, the Keeper will guide you upward. If you are restless and loud, the tower’s voice will turn sharp and you will find yourself lost in the stairwell. The Keeper also gives gifts to those who prove themselves: a feather that can muffle footsteps, a breath that can clear poison from the air, or a whisper that reveals hidden doors. These gifts are rarely given to those who enter the tower seeking only treasure.

At the very top of the tower is the Hall of Winds, where the air is thin and the whispers are loudest. The hall contains a stone table carved with lines like a map of the sky. If you place your hand on the table and listen, the wind will answer with a story of a place far away. It may tell you of a storm brewing in the east, of the movement of clouds over a distant forest, or of a lost path through the mountains. The hall also tests you with a final question: what do you bring back to the world if you carry the wind’s story? The answer is not easy, because the wind changes as you speak. The hall rewards those who speak truthfully and makes those who lie hear only the echo of their own voice.

Those who leave the Tower of the Whispering Winds say they feel lighter, as if a weight was lifted from their shoulders. They often emerge with new directions in their journey and a sense that the dungeon is not only a place of stone and shadow, but also of living whispers. The tower’s story is a reminder that the dungeon has many voices, and some of them are meant to guide rather than deceive. If you choose to follow the wind, you may find that the path ahead is clearer than it was before.
