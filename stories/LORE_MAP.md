# Lore Map: Connections & Thematic Threads

This document maps relationships between quests, regions, NPCs, and factions to show how the world connects.

## The Central Question: What Really Happened?

All major storylines trace back to **The Sundering** — but different factions and quests propose different answers:

### Theories of The Sundering

| Theory | Origin | Evidence |
|--------|--------|----------|
| **Natural Disaster** | Orthodox Codex | Geological records, placating authorities |
| **Deliberate Sabotage** | The Underground | Elder records, historical inconsistencies |
| **Divine Test** | Church of Flame | Faith and theological interpretation |
| **Alien Invasion** | Deepwarriors | Abyssal creatures, survival focus |
| **Scheduled Event** | Wardens of Equilibrium | Cosmic cycles, dimensional monitoring |
| **Unknown Intent** | Shadow Market | "I'll sell my theories to whoever bids highest" |

## Quest Relationships

### Tier 1: Entry Quests (Town-Based)

**The Stolen Mower** (Buchanan)
- **Quest Giver:** Farmer Bevin
- **Faction Connection:** None directly (intentionally low-stakes)
- **Themes:** Absurdity in serious world, unexpected depth
- **Leads To:** Curiosity about Elder technology and artifacts
- **Economy:** Moderate gold reward

**Book Eater's Gyre** (Coloma)
- **Quest Giver:** Master Elira (Codex Society)
- **Faction Connection:** Orthodox Codex (main), The Underground (subplot)
- **Themes:** Moral complexity, empathy for "monsters"
- **Leads To:** Interest in Codex operations and secrets
- **Economy:** High gold, access to Archive

### Tier 2: Mid-Level Quests (Regional Mysteries)

**The Poisoned Well** (Eau Claire)
- **Quest Giver:** Mother Henrika (Church of Flame affiliate)
- **Faction Connection:** Church (investigating), Codex (studying), Wardens (monitoring)
- **Themes:** Environmental danger, dimensional instability
- **Leads To:** Realization that breaches are expanding
- **Economy:** Moderate gold, factional standing

**The Cartographer's Maps** (World)
- **Quest Giver:** Elara the Wanderer (Independent scholar)
- **Faction Connection:** Codex Society (secondary), Shadow Market (grey area)
- **Themes:** Legacy, exploration, history, loss
- **Leads To:** Knowledge of dungeon structure and hidden areas
- **Economy:** Very high gold, access to secret passages

### Tier 3: Advanced Quests (Factional)

**The Forgotten Archive** (Coloma)
- **Quest Giver:** Archivist Tomas (The Underground)
- **Faction Connection:** The Underground (primary), Orthodox Codex (antagonist)
- **Themes:** Truth vs. secrecy, institutional power, conspiracy
- **Leads To:** Major factional conflict, moral choice
- **Economy:** Highest gold, factional alignment shift
- **Incompatible With:** Siding with Orthodox Codex on other quests

**The Deepest Dark** (World)
- **Quest Giver:** Captain Foss (Neutral administrator)
- **Faction Connection:** All factions interested (for different reasons)
- **Themes:** The unknown, cosmic horror, player agency
- **Leads To:** Contact with The Keeper (possibly), world-changing revelation
- **Economy:** Maximum payouts, legendary items
- **Affects:** Every other quest's context and meaning

## Factional Overview & Quest Alignment

### 1. Orthodox Codex Society
**Guiding Principle:** Knowledge should be carefully managed

**Related Quests:**
- Book Eater's Gyre (investigate; they fund it)
- Forbidden Archive (they oppose; you might betray them)
- Deepest Dark (they want artifacts; conflicted)

**NPC Contacts:** Master Elira, High Master Aldrin

**Economic Role:** Fund research expeditions; buy artifacts

### 2. The Underground (Heretical Codex)
**Guiding Principle:** Truth must be revealed

**Related Quests:**
- Forgotten Archive (central; they hire you)
- Book Eater's Gyre (they want the creature studied)
- Deepest Dark (they want evidence of conspiracy)

**NPC Contacts:** Archivist Tomas

**Economic Role:** Pay for "sensitive" expeditions; fund information gathering

### 3. Church of the Sundered Flame
**Guiding Principle:** Dungeon work is divine

**Related Quests:**
- Poisoned Well (they want breach stopped for spiritual reasons)
- Deepest Dark (they believe in cosmic forces)
- Cartographer's Maps (they fund exploration)

**NPC Contacts:** High Priestess Miriam, Mother Henrika

**Economic Role:** Bless expeditions; donate to causes; reward faith-based quests

### 4. Deepwarriors Guild
**Guiding Principle:** Strength and conquest matter

**Related Quests:**
- Deepest Dark (they want to be first to find power)
- Cartographer's Maps (they want dungeon advantage)
- ANY combat-heavy quest (they offer mercenary support)

**NPC Contacts:** Commander Kess

**Economic Role:** Hire adventurers for conquest; buy weapons-grade artifacts

### 5. Shadow Market
**Guiding Principle:** Gold talks

**Related Quests:**
- Cartographer's Maps (bidding on map knowledge)
- Forgotten Archive (buying suppressed information)
- Deepest Dark (betting on expedition success)
- ANY quest (they broker side deals)

**NPC Contacts:** The Widow

**Economic Role:** Fund expeditions at variable rates; broker information; handle black market

### 6. Wardens of Equilibrium
**Guiding Principle:** Some things must stay sealed

**Related Quests:**
- Poisoned Well (they monitor breaches)
- Deepest Dark (they prevent access to hidden areas)
- Book Eater's Gyre (they ensure the creature stays contained)

**NPC Contacts:** Unknown (mysterious)

**Economic Role:** Covert; interfere with quests; sabotage dangerous expeditions

## Trading System Integration

### How Trading Affects Lore

**Economic Stories:**
- **Pooled Resources** — Groups save gold to hire mercenaries or buy artifacts
- **Black Market** — Shadow Market operatives trade in contraband
- **Charity** — Rich adventurers gift starter gold to align with Church
- **Information Bartering** — Knowledge is traded for gold
- **Factional Funding** — Factions pay different rates for quests

### Trading Locations

| Location | Typical Traders | Goods/Services |
|----------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Buchanan | All types | Weapons, armor, information |
| Benton | Herbalists, merchants | Rare herbs, healing supplies |
| Coloma | Scholars, adventurers | Maps, research, artifacts |
| Niles | Prospectors, mercenaries | Ore, mining supplies, combat |
| Taverns | Social networkers | Jobs, rumors, alliances |

## Character Progression Paths

### Path 1: The Scholar
Quests: Book Eater → Cartographer's Maps → Forgotten Archive → Deepest Dark
- Aligned with: The Underground, (secretly) Orthodox Codex
- Economy: Gold from research; selling information
- End Goal: Uncover truth about The Sundering

### Path 2: The Warrior
Quests: Stolen Mower → Poisoned Well → Deepest Dark
- Aligned with: Deepwarriors Guild
- Economy: High combat rewards; mercenary work
- End Goal: Conquer the deepest levels

### Path 3: The Neutral Merchant
Quests: Any; trading facilitates all
- Aligned with: Shadow Market, yourself
- Economy: Arbitrage and brokering
- End Goal: Build personal wealth and power

### Path 4: The Believer
Quests: Poisoned Well → Deepest Dark
- Aligned with: Church of the Sundered Flame
- Economy: Faith-based rewards; community support
- End Goal: Spiritual enlightenment through adventure

### Path 5: The Chaotic Explorer
Quests: All of them in conflicting order
- Aligned with: Everyone and no one
- Economy: Play factions against each other
- End Goal: Survive contradictions; embrace chaos

## The Meta-Arc

### Year 1 (Current)

**Acts:**
1. **Exploration** — Stolen Mower, Book Eater, Cartographer's Maps (learn the world)
2. **Crisis** — Poisoned Well (something is wrong)
3. **Conspiracy** — Forgotten Archive (nothing is what it seems)
4. **Revelation** — Deepest Dark (the truth, whatever it is)

### Implied Year 2+

- **Factional War** — The Underground goes public; society fragments
- **Dimensional Crisis** — More breaches open; Wardens can't suppress them
- **The Keeper's Intent** — Whatever was contacted in the depths awakens
- **New Powers** — Players who reached the depths gain incomprehensible abilities
- **The World Changes** — The dungeon expands, transforms, or reveals its true nature

## Thematic Layers

### Layer 1: Adventure (Surface)
- "Let me explore dungeons and get treasure"
- Satisfied by: Stolen Mower, Book Eater, core combat

### Layer 2: Mystery (Intermediate)
- "What happened to cause The Sundering? Who were the Elder?"
- Satisfied by: Cartographer's Maps, Poisoned Well

### Layer 3: Politics (Deeper)
- "Which faction's version of truth should I support?"
- Satisfied by: Forgotten Archive, all the factional connections

### Layer 4: Cosmic (Deepest)
- "What is The Keeper? What is the dungeon really? Am I just a piece in someone's game?"
- Satisfied by: Deepest Dark, end-game revelations
