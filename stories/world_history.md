# World History: The Dungeon of Doom

## Before the Sundering

Millennia ago, the lands now known as the Dungeon of Doom were a thriving civilization. The ancient peoples possessed knowledge of magic, engineering, and the ability to harness the very essence of reality itself. Cities rose and fell, empires waxed and waned, but the core of this civilization remained eternal — protected by a network of underground vaults and passages.

## The Sundering

Three centuries past, a catastrophic magical event known as "The Sundering" fractured the barrier between the material plane and the Abyss. What emerged was chaos incarnate: malevolent entities, twisted creatures, and corrupted magic that seeped into every stone and crevice of the land.

The surface civilizations were devastated. Most perished or fled to distant lands. But the underground vault system, sealed by ancient protections, remained intact. Or so the survivors thought.

## The Long Underground

Survivors sheltered in the vault corridors for generations, slowly uncovering treasures and knowledge from the fallen civilization. But the vaults were never meant to be lived in forever. Slowly, the seals weakened. Monsters began to emerge from the deepest chambers.

Eventually, those who remained fought their way back to the surface, establishing fortified settlements across the land — towns like Buchanan, Benton, Coloma, and Niles became bastions of humanity against the darkness below.

## The Present Age

Today, the dungeons are a known threat and a source of wealth. Adventurers delve into the depths seeking treasure, knowledge, and glory. Towns have grown around dungeon entrances, establishing trade routes for dungeon goods and forming adventurer's guilds.

The vaults still hold secrets. Some say the original civilization's greatest works lie dormant in the deepest chambers. Others whisper that something far more sinister dwells below — something that the ancient seals were meant to *contain*, not protect.

The Dungeon of Doom remains unconquered. And every day, new adventurers enter its depths.
