# The Fallen City of Coloma

Coloma was a city of gold and marble, its name whispered as a place of easy wealth. Its market squares were lined with stalls selling silk, spices, and trinkets wrought by master craftsmen. The river that fed the city glittered with reflected lantern light each night. Traders came from faraway towns because the coins that changed hands in Coloma were said to bring luck. Nobles kept their ledgers in towers with golden domes. Children grew up hearing tales of silver-lined fountains and friendly spirits that guarded the streets. The city had its own pride, and that pride was built on the sound of money cascading louder than any bell.

When the curse fell, the light faded and the city fractured. The marble turned gray, then cracked. The river ran backward and the fountains began to bleed black water. The market stalls froze in the act of bargaining, their merchants vanishing into shadows. Statues wept the same dark fluid that now puddled in the plazas. The city’s streets became a maze of flooded halls and ruined bridges. The roar of the crowd was replaced by the drip of water on cold stone. The curse did not spare the proudest tower or the smallest courtyard.

The ruins are now a labyrinth of market stalls and flooded halls. Silver statues weep black water, and the ghosts of traders still haggle in empty corridors. The ghosts are not all malicious, but they are trapped in their last bargaining moments. Some of them still offer you cursed deals in muffled tones. The halls echo with the clink of coins and the whispers of lost bargains. Every corner seems to contain a risk wrapped in a bargain. The city itself remembers the easy wealth it once offered, and that memory is the most dangerous part of all. It feeds the traps and guides them toward greedy hands.

The most dangerous treasure is the city’s own currency: cursed coins that seem to multiply when you look away. They draw hungry things from the shadows, and the shadows are patient. Adventurers have died clutching pockets full of coins while the curse slowly tightened around them. It is not always the monsters that kill you; sometimes it is your own eyes. One wrong glance at a shimmering pile can make the walls shift. The coins themselves seem to whisper. They make promises of riches, power, and influence. The trick is to see through them to the cost behind the sheen.

Only those who can keep their wits and their pockets straight will leave Coloma with more than just stories. Those survivors speak of a path that avoids the largest piles of gold. They speak of silence and careful steps. They also speak of a hidden vault beneath the city that might contain the key to undoing the curse. It is guarded by a creature that knows every merchant’s lie. Some say the key is a single honest coin. Others say it is the memory of a bargain refused. In any case, the story of Coloma is not only one of wealth lost, but also of the cunning needed to survive its hauntings.
