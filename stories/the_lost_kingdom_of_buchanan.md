# The Lost Kingdom of Buchanan

Buchanan was once a kingdom of mills, orchards, and watchtowers. Its people sailed the rivers and made offerings to the guardian spirits of the woods. The city was famous for its blue banners and the choir that sang at every dawn. Children learned to read by lantern light and dreamt of distant journeys. The fields were rich and the taverns were warm with laughter. None of them expected the ground to shift beneath their feet. In quiet moments, the elders whispered about the old pact with the harvest spirits. They knew the balance was fragile, but they believed it would hold.

When the curse fell, the light faded and the kingdom fractured. The noble houses disappeared into the earth, leaving behind fortified gates and crumbling bridges. The river changed course overnight and the orchards turned to thorn and gray moss. The watchtowers became ruins that still groaned when wind passed through them. People fled toward the old road, but many were swallowed before they reached the forest. Only a handful survived to tell the tale of how the earth opened. In the maps that remain, Buchanan appears as a rumor rather than a place. The old songs speak of a city lost beneath the curse.

There are stories of a hidden vault under the old capital, filled with silver plates and enchanted armor. The vault is said to lie beneath the throne hall, behind a wall that only opens for those who remember the kingdom's true name. Those who seek it must pass through halls of glass and shadow. Gargoyles perch on ruined ledges and their eyes glow when greed draws near. The floors shift like living creatures, and the air is thick with the perfume of lost spices. Only the brave or foolish attempt the vault, and very few return to tell of it. The treasure is not just coins, but the memory of what Buchanan once was.

Some older survivors claim the crown of Buchanan was never truly lost. They say it waits in a quiet place where the first river met the sea. The crown is rumored to glow faintly when a willing heart passes by. It is not only a symbol of power, but a key to the kingdom's forgotten songs. Those who hear the song say it can soothe the land and call the guardian spirits back. Many dismiss the story as a bedtime tale, but the best adventurers keep it in mind. The ground around ancient ruins still hums with that promise. If the crown is ever found, it may be the first step toward restoring what the curse took. That promise, fragile though it seems, lives in the whisper of the wind over ruined stones.

Survivors also claim a hidden road of light exists beneath the city, visible only to those who carry no greed in their hand. It is said to be guarded by a pair of stone stags with eyes like moonlight. Only a traveler who can answer their silent question may pass. The stag guardians do not ask for gold; they ask for the memory of why you began your journey. Those who fail are sent back into the maze. Those who succeed are brought to an underground grove where the old land still remembers rain. In that grove is a pool of water so clear you can see all the versions of Buchanan that might still be. The story of Buchanan ends only when someone carries its lost crown back into the sunlight.
