# The Stolen Mower: A Comedy of Error

## Quest Giver

**Farmer Bevin** — An aging farmer from the outskirts of Buchanan, perpetually frustrated by fate.

## The Quest Hook

Farmer Bevin's prized possession — a mechanical mower, looted from the Elder Civilization and repurposed for farmwork — was stolen by a gang of goblin scramblers three days ago. He's offering a substantial reward to get it back.

The twist? The mower is nearly impossible to operate, frequently breaks down, and Bevin himself isn't sure why the goblins would want it. This makes the entire quest feel absurd.

## The Lore

### A Farmer's Obsession

Bevin found the mower on a solo dungeon run decades ago. Most adventurers would have left it — it's heavy, bulky, and the glyphs are undeciphered. But Bevin recognized potential. By trial and error over years, he figured out how to operate the basic mechanisms.

The mower cuts grass at an inhuman efficiency. It also hums ominously, sometimes sparks shoot from it, and locals believe it's cursed. But Bevin loves it. It made his farm the most productive in the region, and he made his fortune because of it.

### The Theft

Three days ago, goblins raided his farm at night. They weren't interested in crops, livestock, or valuables. They wanted the mower specifically. They came with purpose, took only the mower, and disappeared into the forest.

This is unusual. Goblins don't steal technological items — they have no use for them. Bevin suspects someone sent them. Someone who wants the mower back.

### Hidden Depth

There's a possibility that the mower is more significant than it appears. Scholars believe it's an Elder agricultural automation device. But some whisper that it might be something else — monitoring equipment, a beacon, or a lock mechanism for something buried beneath the farmland.

## Possible Outcomes

1. **Retrieve the mower directly** — Adventurers track the goblins to their warren and fight for the mower. Straightforward but dangerous.

2. **Discover who hired the goblins** — Leads to black-market interests that want the mower for unknown purposes. This path opens larger conspiracies.

3. **Repair the mower** — If broken during retrieval, adventurers must find parts or knowledge to fix it. This could lead to deeper vault exploration.

4. **Learn the mower's true purpose** — Grants significant lore points and opens multi-phase quests related to Elder technology.

## Rewards

- **Gold** — Standard payment from Bevin
- **Mower Access Pass** — Farmers who complete the quest can use the mower for trading/crafting
- **Salvage Rights** — Access to Bevin's collection of other Elder artifacts he's recovered over the years
- **The Mechanism Key** — An item that hints at deeper mysteries related to Elder technology

## Connections to Larger Lore

This quest could connect to:
- Investigations into who's buying Elder tech on the black market
- The mysterious purpose of the Codex Society's higher projects
- Theories about whether the Elder Civilization is still somehow active

## Humor Value

Admittedly, this quest is meant to be somewhat tongue-in-cheek. A farmer, a magical mower, and goblins that don't make sense create an intentional narrative absurdity that contrasts with the darker, more serious quests in the world.
