# The Forgotten Archive: A Scholar's Obsession

## Quest Giver

**Archivist Tomas** — A recluse scholar who lives in the Coloma Scholar's Archive. He's been researching the Elder Civilization for thirty years and is convinced the deepest library vaults hold the key to understanding why The Sundering happened.

## The Quest Hook

Tomas has managed to decipher fragments of Elder maps indicating a **Forbidden Library** exists somewhere in the dungeon's middle levels. He's offering a significant reward to anyone who can:

1. Navigate there safely
2. Return with intact scrolls or tablets
3. Keep the existence of the library secret from the Codex Society

The latter requirement is suspicious and intriguing.

## The Lore

### The Archive Obsession

Tomas wasn't always reclusive. He was once a prominent member of the Codex Society. But twenty years ago, he made a discovery in an old vault that changed everything. He claims to have found evidence that The Sundering wasn't *natural* — it was *orchestrated*. Someone, or something, purposefully tore the barrier.

The Society laughed him out. They called it conspiracy theory. But Tomas knows what he saw.

### The Forbidden Library

In the fragments he's translated, there are references to a "Repository of Truths" — a library that the Elder Civilization specifically sealed away from even their closest scholars. The seals on this library are still intact, Tomas believes. And he thinks the truth about The Sundering lies within.

But there's a problem: the library is *actively defended* by magical constructs the Elders left behind. These aren't the degraded guardians of other vaults — these still have full power. Opening the library might set something catastrophic free.

### Tomas's Secret

Tomas is working on behalf of a faction *within* the Codex Society that disagrees with the mainstream consensus. This underground group believes the public should know the full truth about The Sundering, even if it's dangerous knowledge. That's why he's hiring outside adventurers rather than using official channels — the orthodox Society would confiscate anything he retrieves.

## The Dungeon: The Forbidden Library

### The Approach

The adventurer must navigate through mid-level dungeon floors to find a hidden entrance. The map fragments Tomas provides are partially correct but deliberately misdirected — either by time's passage or by Tomas himself (unclear which).

The entrance is disguised as a sealed wall that requires solving an Elder puzzle or finding a specific key item.

### The Interior

The library is vast but architecturally strange. Corridors curve impossibly. Rooms seem to exist in multiple places simultaneously. This is deliberate Elder architecture, designed to be impossible to navigate without the right knowledge or magical keys.

Books line walls but are written in Elder script. Some are physical tomes. Others are crystals that project images and text into the air. Strange mechanisms control access to different sections.

### The Guardians

**Sentrysprites** — Constructs of pure magical energy in humanoid form. They're programmed to prevent anyone from accessing the sealed archive specifically. They don't attack indiscriminately but will relentlessly pursue anyone attempting to breach the innermost vault.

They're intelligent enough to be reasoned with, but their programming is absolute.

### The Truth

Inside the sealed archive, adventurers find several categories of records:

1. **Technical Logs** — Records of Elder experiments with planar boundaries. The entries become increasingly panicked and fragmented.

2. **The Breach Event** — A series of entries describing a deliberate experiment that went catastrophically wrong. Some entries suggest it might have been intentional sabotage. Others suggest it was an accident with world-ending consequences.

3. **The Contingency** — Final entries indicating the Elder Civilization knew what was coming. They created the vaults as safe houses and left messages for whoever might survive.

4. **The Warning** — Most ominously, there are recent entries. Written in Elder script but *recently* — within the last year. As if something in the vault is still writing, still recording. Still *watching*.

## Possible Outcomes

1. **Public the Truth** — Return everything to Tomas's underground faction. They publish it. Society is thrown into chaos. Multiple factions vie for control of the dungeon.

2. **Suppress the Information** — Turn Tomas in to the mainstream Codex Society. They seize the materials and bury them. You're rewarded but become known as someone who betrays confidants.

3. **Investigate Further** — Someone or something is writing in the vault. Track down who/what. This could lead to discovering that the Elder Civilization might not be as extinct as everyone believes.

4. **The Artifact** — One entry describes a device that could monitor planar boundaries. If you can find it and understand it, you might be able to prevent another Sundering from ever happening. Alternatively, you might accidentally trigger one.

## Rewards

- **Gold** — Substantial payment from Tomas
- **Knowledge** — Permanent buff to lore/research skills
- **The Key** — A device that grants access to restricted library sections
- **The Secret** — Knowledge that rewrites the world's history. Depending on what you do with it, this could be worth gold to different factions, or priceless for personal knowledge

## Connections to Larger World

- Connects to the mysterious "Keeper" figure and the idea that something intelligent may still dwell in the deepest vaults
- Opens the possibility that The Sundering could happen again
- Reveals factional conflicts within the Codex Society itself
- Could lead to meta-quests about *why* someone is still writing in the sealed vault
- Might introduce the concept that the dungeon itself could be conscious or controlled

## Themes

- The danger of hidden knowledge
- Institutional power and suppression of truth
- The possibility that history isn't what we're told
- That our greatest enemy might be ourselves (the Sundering was self-inflicted)
