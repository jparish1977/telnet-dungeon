# The Deepest Dark: A Horror Below

## Quest Giver

**Captain Foss** — The grizzled garrison commander of Buchanan. He's seen a lot but is clearly worried about something he's not sharing yet.

## The Quest Hook

Over the past weeks, adventuring parties have been returning from deep dungeon expeditions with reports of something *wrong*. Not specific dangers or monsters — they describe a feeling. A presence. A sense of being *watched* by something vast and alien.

More concerning: some experienced adventurers have gone *deep* and haven't returned. Not dead in combat (no bodies, no wreckage). Just... gone. Disappeared into lower floors.

Captain Foss is hiring top adventurers to investigate what's happening in the highest floor numbers of the dungeon. He's offering expedition funding and major rewards, but he's also honest: "I don't know what's down there. I don't know if you can come back up."

## The Lore

### The Silence

For years, experienced adventurers have been mapping the deepest dungeon floors. They typically find plenty of danger but also predictable patterns — floors have architecture, monsters with types, treasure in logical places.

But recently, expeditions to the deepest floors report something different: *silence*. The air changes. Monster activity drops to zero. It's as if the dungeon *ends* somehow, or transitions to a different reality.

And there's this presence.

### The Void

Adventurers struggling to describe the presence use terms like:
- "It has no shape but I could *feel* it in the darkness"
- "Like the dungeon itself is looking at you"
- "An intelligence so large I can't comprehend it. I was an *ant* to it."
- "It wasn't hostile. But it was *aware*."

### Missing Parties

The disappearances aren't random:

1. **The Delvers' Guild Expedition** — A paid team sent to confirm rumors of a mythical treasure. No contact in 3 weeks.
2. **The Scholar's Quest** — Master Elira sent an expedition to continue research. They didn't return.
3. **The Knight's Fellowship** — A high-level group seeking glory. Last report: "We've found something. Going deeper."

What connects them: they all continued deeper than standard adventurers typically go. They all deliberately sought the *bottom* of the dungeon.

### The Keepe

Deeper whispers speak of something called **The Keeper**. An entity that allegedly dwells in the absolute depths of the dungeon. The Keeper is said to:

- Guard the deepest secrets of the Elder Civilization
- Possess wisdom or power incomprehensible to mortals
- Be *waiting* for someone, or something
- Have existed since before The Sundering

Most people think it's just mythology. But several missing parties were specifically looking for The Keeper.

## The Expedition

### The Descent

To reach the absolute depths, adventurers must:

1. Navigate known dungeon floors (increasingly dangerous)
2. Breach the "Floor of Silence" — a level where all sound is absorbed
3. Cross the "Abyss Threshold" — where Abyssal presence becomes overwhelming
4. Descend to Floor 99+ — where the dungeon *changes*

At higher floors, normal rules break down:
- Gravity fluctuates
- Time feels distorted (has 5 minutes passed or 5 hours?)
- Monsters are rarer but infinitely more dangerous
- Architecture becomes non-Euclidean
- Reality itself feels thin

### The Void Spaces

Deepest floors aren't fully solid. There are sections that feel like voids — spaces where the dungeon hasn't been fully *rendered*, like unfinished creation. Walking into a void space risks:

- Falling into absolute darkness
- Losing sense of direction
- Encountering things that *shouldn't exist* — fragments of other realities?

### The Messages

Strangely, on the deepest floors, there are *messages*. Written in Elder script, sometimes in more recent handwriting. Scattered across walls and stones:

- "The bottom was never meant to be reached"
- "It remembers us"
- "We are still catalogued in its thoughts"
- "The Keeper asks: are you ready?"
- "(Fresh handwriting)"Do not go deeper"

## Possible Encounters

### The Vanished Expeditions

Players might encounter remnants of missing expeditions:

- A survivor, barely sane, who's been surviving alone in the deep
- Bodies in stasis, preserved by unknown means
- Equipment scattered with no bodies suggest they were... taken
- A campsite with a journal explaining their last thoughts

### The Keeper's Forms

What adventurers encounter in the deepest levels is ambiguous:

1. **It might be an entity** — a consciousness vast enough that it can't be perceived directly
2. **It might be the dungeon itself** — conscious, aware, possibly benevolent
3. **It might be multiple things** — The Keeper is a title, not a single being
4. **It might be a trap** — what seems like an intelligence might be an automated Elder system
5. **It might be calling for specific adventurers** — not just anyone, but someone with particular qualities

### The Choice

When (if) adventurers reach the absolute bottom, they'll face a choice:

1. **Leave** — Return to the surface and report what they've found
2. **Stay** — The Keeper asks them to remain and help with something
3. **Ascend** — Attempt to go even deeper (into the Abyss? Into another dungeon?)
4. **Negotiate** — The Keeper seems willing to communicate
5. **Fight** — For some reason, combat seems... possible, though the Keeper is clearly more powerful

## Rewards

- **Gold** — Max payout from any quest source
- **The Keeper's Mark** — A mysterious item that grants unknown benefits
- **Knowledge** — What adventurers learn will reshape the world
- **Allies in the Deep** — The Keeper might offer assistance or deals to those who haven't fled

## The Consequences

This isn't a quest with a clean ending. Whatever happens at the bottom has world-changing implications:

- If The Keeper is hostile, it could represent an existential threat
- If it's helpful, it might offer power or knowledge too dangerous to possess
- If it's neutral, it might simply be cataloguing adventurers for future use
- If it's something else entirely, players get to discover what

## Themes

- Cosmic horror balanced with wonder
- The unknowable
- Morality in the face of the incomprehensible
- Whether some things should be discovered
- The Elder Civilization's final mystery
- What awaits those who delve too deep

## Connection to Meta-Lore

The deepest dungeon levels could be:

- An alive Elder artifact
- A imprisoned god or entity
- The heart of the breach between worlds
- A simulation or memory of the Elder Civilization
- Something that existed before the Elder Civilization
- The *reason* the old civilization fell

This quest is intentionally left open-ended. Different adventurers might face different versions of The Keeper. What it is depends partly on who reaches it and what they bring with them.
