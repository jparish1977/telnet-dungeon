# The Poisoned Well: Eau Claire Mystery

## Quest Giver

**Mother Henrika** — A traveling healer who has set up temporary residence in the Eau Claire settlement. She's concerned and frustrated.

## The Quest Hook

The water wells in Eau Claire have become contaminated with an unknown substance. People who drink it report:

- Vivid, disturbing dreams
- Temporary paralysis  
- Seeing things that aren't there
- In severe cases, complete loss of consciousness

Henrika has quarantined the wells and is rationing clean water from a single upwind source, but it's not sustainable. She's offering payment to anyone who can:

1. Find the source of the contamination
2. Neutralize or remove it
3. Prevent it from happening again

## The Lore

### The Timing

The contamination started roughly a month ago. Before then, Eau Claire had never had water problems — it sits on natural springs and has underground aquifers. The contamination seems almost *introduced* rather than natural.

### The Substance

Henrika has limited resources for analysis, but she's determined that the substance is *magical in nature*. It's not a poison in the traditional sense. It's something that interferes with the mind and senses.

Her hypothesis: *it's not human-made*. The magical signature matches descriptions of Abyssal magic — the kind of corruption that seeps through from beyond the barrier The Sundering created.

### The Connection

A few months ago, there were reports of unusual tremors in the region. Not earthquakes exactly, but rhythmic vibrations deep underground. They started them stopped abruptly just before the water contamination began.

What if something broke through into the underground aquifer system?

## Investigation Path

### The Deep Well

There's an old well in Eau Claire that goes *very* deep — some say it reaches into caverns that connect to the main dungeon system. The newer wells draw from shallower springs, but the deep well was sealed/abandoned after an incident years ago.

Adventurers investigating might find that the seal is weakened. Something is seeping through.

### The Underground River

If players follow the water sources deeper, they might discover an underground river that flows from the dungeon depths toward Eau Claire. This river seems to originate from an area of the dungeon known for high Abyssal presence.

The contamination is likely traveling through this river.

### The Portal

Deeper still, players might find evidence that a *breach* was made in dimensional barriers. Something from the Abyss has created a kind of wound that allows its essence to leak through. The contamination is that essence infecting the water.

## Possible Solutions

1. **Plug the Breach** — Use magical seals, Elder technology, or brute-force methods to close the breach. Risky because you're dealing with Abyssal magic.

2. **Kill the Source** — If something is *generating* the contamination (a creature, a spirit, a construct), kill it. The water might naturally cleanse itself afterward.

3. **Redirect the Flow** — Divert the underground river so it doesn't flow toward Eau Claire. Massive effort but might be permanent.

4. **Purification Ritual** — Work with local healers/shamans to perform a ritual that cleanses the water. Temporary fix unless done regularly.

5. **Adaptation** — Instead of fixing the water, develop a solution (boiling, magical filtration) that Eau Claire can maintain long-term.

## Rewards

- **Gold** — From Henrika and the village
- **Healer's Mark** — A buff that grants poison resistance and +1 to healing potions
- **Access to Deep Well** — The route to the dungeon depths becomes a fast travel option
- **Elder Seal Knowledge** — If you seal the breach, you learn about Elder barrier magic

## Complications

- Attempting to seal the breach might anger something *on the other side*
- The contamination might not be accidental — someone might have *deliberately* created the breach
- Killing the source creature might alert larger, worse things to the area
- Redirecting the river might have ecological consequences downriver

## Connections to Larger World

- Suggests that The Sundering isn't stable — new breaches might form
- Implies something is actively trying to expand Abyssal influence
- Could lead to quests about strengthening dimensional barriers
- Might reveal that certain factions *want* more breaches (for power, knowledge, or chaos)

## Follow-ups

If players succeed but temporary solution:
- "The contamination is returning. Henrika fears the breach is reopening."
- Could lead to repeat investigations or permanent solution quests

If players align with anti-Abyssal factions:
- Doors open with the Church of the Sundered Flame or barrier-focused groups

If players discover deliberate sabotage:
- Major conspiracy quests could open about who wants breaches and why
